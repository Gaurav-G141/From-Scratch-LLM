--- 1 ---
Claim: For all integers n, n^2 ≥ n.
Proof: n^2 - n = n(n-1) which is nonnegative because product of two integers that are either both ≥1 or both ≤0... wait if n=0 then 0. If n=1 then 0. If n≥2 then n and n-1 positive. If n negative then n negative and n-1 more negative so product positive. So ≥0.

--- 2 ---
Claim: A ⊆ B iff for all x, x∈A implies x∈B.
Proof: Forward: if A subset B and x in A then x in B. Backward: suppose every x in A is in B, that means A is inside B so A subset B.

--- 24 ---
